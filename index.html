Thoughts on Progressive Enhancement

Progressive enhancement is an approach in modern web development to prioritize the visibility of a website’s content regardless of a user’s Internet connection or web browser. Not all users have access to fast Internet speeds or numerous web browsers, so web developers who implement progressive enhancement in his or her website ensure that the content of the site will always be visible in spite of Internet speed or web browser type. Through increased Internet bandwidth and browser accessibility, the website’s content becomes progressively enhanced by more nuanced and technologically meticulous layers of presentation and features. The core principles of progressive enhancement are as follows: 1) At the minimum, the basic content and basic functionality of a website must be accessible. 2) The markup language, or HTML, should contain all of the content. 3) The CSS, which develops the style of the content, must be linked externally. 4) The JavaScript, or any other programming language used to determine the behavior or functionality of the website, must not interfere with the accessibility of the content in any way and must also be externally linked. 5) The user’s choice of browser must be honored.
